Subject Pronouns, Object Pronouns, Possessive Adjectives & Pronouns

Part 1 - Multiple Choice (Questions 1-6)

Choose the correct option.

Question 1
Emma spoke to John after class, but ___ did not understand ___ message clearly.
Your answer:
Correct answer: he / her

Why: First blank = SUBJECT pronoun (he does action). Second blank = possessive ADJECTIVE before noun (her message).

Examples:
- He read her email
- She answered his call
- They understood our question
Question 2
This jacket is not ___, because ___ is still in my car today.
Your answer:
Correct answer: mine / mine

Why: Both blanks need possessive PRONOUN (stands alone). "Is not mine" / "mine is" - no noun after!

Examples:
- This is not mine. Mine is new
- That's not yours. Yours is red
- It's not his. His is bigger
Question 3
Sarah likes her new job, but ___ says ___ manager is very strict.
Your answer:
Correct answer: she / her

Why: First blank = SUBJECT pronoun (she does action "says"). Second blank = possessive ADJECTIVE before noun (her manager).

Examples:
- She likes her boss
- He loves his team
- They enjoy their work
Question 4
I sent the documents to Mark, but ___ did not reply to ___ email.
Your answer:
Correct answer: he / my

Why: First blank = SUBJECT pronoun (he does action). Second blank = possessive ADJECTIVE before noun (my email).

Examples:
- He answered my message
- She read my letter
- They ignored my calls
Question 5
The children forgot ___ backpacks, so ___ parents had to return home.
Your answer:
Correct answer: their / their

Why: Both blanks need possessive ADJECTIVE before nouns (their backpacks / their parents). Use "their" before both!

Examples:
- Their bags are heavy
- Their parents are nice
- Their house is big
Question 6
That seat is not ___, because ___ is already taken near the window.
Your answer:
Correct answer: yours / yours

Why: Both blanks need possessive PRONOUN (stands alone). "Is not yours" / "yours is" - no noun after!

Examples:
- This is not yours. Yours is there
- That's not mine. Mine is here
- It's not his. His is outside

Part 2 - Fill in the Blanks (Questions 7-12)

Complete the paragraph by selecting the correct pronouns from the dropdowns.

Yesterday, John and Sarah went to the office early because had an important meeting. John brought laptop, but Sarah forgot at home. The manager spoke to about the project and asked to finish it by Friday. At the end of the meeting, the documents were , not the manager's.

Explanations for Questions 7-12

Question 7
Correct answer: they

Why: "John and Sarah" = two people (plural). Subject pronoun = "they" (does the action "had").

Examples:
- They work together
- They live nearby
- They study English
Question 8
Correct answer: his

Why: Need possessive ADJECTIVE before noun "laptop". His + noun (NOT "hers" which is possessive pronoun).

Examples:
- His computer is new
- His phone is old
- His car is fast
Question 9
Correct answer: hers

Why: Need possessive PRONOUN (stands alone, no noun after). "Forgot hers" = forgot her laptop.

Examples:
- She forgot hers
- He lost his
- They brought theirs
Question 10
Correct answer: them

Why: After preposition "to", use OBJECT pronoun! "Spoke to them" (NOT "to they").

Examples:
- He spoke to them
- She talked to them
- I listened to them
Question 11
Correct answer: them

Why: After verb "asked", use OBJECT pronoun! "Asked them" (NOT "asked they").

Examples:
- She asked them
- He told them
- I helped them
Question 12
Correct answer: theirs

Why: Need possessive PRONOUN (stands alone). "Were theirs" = were their documents (no noun after).

Examples:
- The books are theirs
- The car is ours
- The house is mine
